Republic of Sierra Leone Armed Forces Activities & Events

Chief of Defence Staff Lieutenant General PK Lavahun delivering a Statement


The Republic of Sierra Leone Armed Forces in collaboration with the Elsie Initiative Fund and the United Nations Women on Wednesday 30 November 2022 launched the 2022 Barrier Assessment Report on Measuring Opportunities for Women in Peace Operations, at the Occasion Resort Lakka, Freetown.


In her declaration of purpose Lieutenant Patricia Iyesatta Saio Sheriff said, the purpose of the was to witness the 2022 Barrier Assessment Report on Measuring Opportunities for Women in peace Operations. The assessment she said, was done by the United Nations Women, Cornell University, local consultants and the RSLAF of which the project lasted for 18 months. Despite the direct benefits RSLAF will gain in the future, it has already benefited indirectly by enhancing partnership with UN Women, a strategic partnership with the Geneva Centre for security Sector Governance and strengthening the relationships with the Canadian High Commission in Ghana. The Chief of Defence Staff, Lieutenant General Peter K Lavahun in his statement, said, the Ministry of Defence and the Republic of Sierra Leone Armed Forces expressed their interest to the Elsie secretariat steering Committee was motivated by their commitment to overcome gender hierarchies and stereotypes, and the need to address systemic and structural barriers with the support of the Elsie Fund. “We thought it to partner with the UN women Sierra Leone to conduct assessment to identify the main factors impeding the deployment of uniformed women in peace operations,” he stated. He also, commended the UN Women Country Office in Sierra Leone for their commitments, collaboration, partnership and support to the RSLAF for their tireless efforts towards the launching and publication of the 2022 Barrier Assessment Report.
